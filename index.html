Documentary Filmmaking Workshop

“Documenting Sustainability and Happiness in Finland”

Dates: November 11–16, 2025
Location: To be confirmed
Tutor: Martin Fazeli – Director & Filmmaker

Introduction

Finland is globally celebrated for its sustainable practices, robust welfare systems, and strong sense of collective well-being. Ranked as the world’s happiest country for several years, Finland provides a unique context for this workshop: “Documenting Sustainability and Happiness in Finland.”

Led by acclaimed filmmaker Martin Fazeli, this 6-day hands-on workshop provides participants with the tools and mentorship to create short documentaries (5–10 minutes) exploring the relationship between sustainability and happiness.

Workshop Overview

  • Duration: 6 Days (Nov 11–16, 2025)
  • Format: In-person, intensive, project-based
  • Final Outcome: Group-created short documentary films
  • Participants: Emerging filmmakers, artists, students, environmentalists
  • Focus: Sustainability, welfare, and the Finnish model of happiness

Benefits for the Finnish Cultural Environment

  1. Promoting Culturally Relevant Storytelling: Empowering participants to tell Finnish stories grounded in local communities.
  2. Supporting Emerging Artists: Strengthening Finland’s creative ecosystem with mentorship and collaboration.
  3. Fostering Interdisciplinary Collaboration: Connecting art, education, and sustainability.
  4. Creating a Cultural Archive: Producing a collection of contemporary Finnish life and values.
  5. Encouraging Public Engagement: Making art participatory through screenings and discussions.

Learning Objectives

  • Understand documentary theory and ethics
  • Develop technical skills: camera, sound, and editing
  • Produce short thematic films in teams
  • Explore links between sustainability, welfare, and happiness

Detailed Schedule

Day 1: Introduction, screenings, and topic brainstorming

Day 2: Research, narrative structures, and pre-production

Day 3: Field interviews and mentoring

Day 4: B-roll filming and rough editing

Day 5: Editing and peer feedback

Day 6: Final edits and screening preparation

Day 7: Public screening and discussion

Thematic Focus

  • Green urban development and eco-living
  • Education and mental well-being
  • Sustainable lifestyles and circular economy
  • Equality and welfare in daily life

Potential Collaborations

  • Municipalities and NGOs for sustainability networks
  • Universities and film schools for outreach
  • Cultural and environmental foundations for funding
  • Media partners for visibility and support

Conclusion

“Documenting Sustainability and Happiness in Finland” is a platform for creativity, education, and reflection. With guidance from Martin Fazeli, participants will not only gain filmmaking skills but also contribute to Finland’s evolving cultural dialogue about sustainability and happiness.

Director’s Bio – Hossein Martin Fazeli

Hossein Martin Fazeli is an award-winning director with over two decades of experience producing and directing films worldwide. His works have aired on major networks such as CBC, BBC, ARTE, and Canal+, and have received 45 international awards. Fazeli has collaborated with the UNDP, European Commission, and other organizations on projects addressing human rights, women’s rights, and social justice. He was selected by the Sundance Institute for the International Filmmakers Award in 2008. He has lectured globally at institutions including SFU (Canada), SOAS (UK), Aarhus University (Denmark), and the European Film Academy.
