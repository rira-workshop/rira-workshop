Documentary Filmmaking Workshop

“CINEMATIC ACTIVISM”

Dates: November 11–16, 2025
Location: Helsinki
Tutor: Martin Fazeli – Director & Filmmaker

Introduction

Documentary film is one of the most powerful mediums for truth-telling, critical reflection, and social transformation. Unlike fiction, documentary embraces the raw and complex realities of the world, giving voice to those who are often unheard and shedding light on stories that mainstream narratives may overlook. It holds a mirror to society, revealing both its beauty and its contradictions, its achievements and its injustices. In an era marked by deep social, environmental, and political challenges, documen- tary filmmaking enables individuals and communities to express their lived experiences, advocate for justice, and inspire change. Through intimate storytelling and compelling visual language, it connects people across backgrounds and cultures, fostering empathy,awareness, and civic responsibility.— “CINEMATIC ACTIVISM.”

Led by acclaimed filmmaker Martin Fazeli, this 6-day intensive hands-on program offers participants the opportunity to create short documentaries.

Workshop Overview

  • Duration: 6 Days (Nov 11–16, 2025)
  • Format: In-person, project-based
  • Outcome: Short group-created documentary films
  • Participants: Filmmakers, artists, students, environmentalists
  • Focus: Sustainability, welfare, and Finland’s model of happiness

Benefits for Finland’s Cultural Landscape

  1. Culturally Relevant Storytelling: Empowering participants to create films rooted in Finnish communities and values.
  2. Supporting Emerging Artists: Mentorship that strengthens Finland’s creative ecosystem.
  3. Interdisciplinary Collaboration: Connecting artists, educators, and environmentalists.
  4. Cultural Archive Creation: Documenting Finland’s evolving identity.
  5. Public Engagement: Bringing art closer to communities through screenings and discussions.

Learning Objectives

  • Understand documentary ethics and storytelling structure
  • Develop technical filmmaking and editing skills
  • Collaborate to produce short thematic documentaries
  • Explore how sustainability and welfare intersect with happiness

Workshop Schedule

Day 1 (Nov 11): Welcome, screenings, group introductions, lecture on ethics and purpose

Day 2 (Nov 12): Research, narrative structures, interview techniques, pre-production planning

Day 3 (Nov 13): Field interviews and one-on-one mentoring with Martin Fazeli

Day 4 (Nov 14): Filming b-roll, visual storytelling, rough editing begins

Day 5 (Nov 15): Editing workshop, feedback from mentor and peers

Day 6 (Nov 16): Final edits, sound mix, screening & discussion

Thematic Focus

  • Green urban development & eco-living
  • Education and mental well-being
  • Sustainable lifestyles and circular economy
  • Welfare, equality, and daily happiness

Collaborations

  • Municipalities & NGOs for local partnerships
  • Film schools & universities for outreach
  • Cultural & environmental foundations for funding
  • Media partners for visibility and technical support

Director’s Bio – Hossein Martin Fazeli

Hossein Martin Fazeli is an award-winning director with over two decades of experience producing and directing fiction and non-fiction films. His works have aired on CBC, BBC, ARTE, and Canal+, and received over 45 international awards. He has collaborated with the UNDP, European Commission, and Nonviolence International on projects addressing human rights, women’s rights, and minority issues.

He was selected by the Sundance Institute for the International Filmmakers Award (2008) and has lectured at SFU (Canada), SOAS (UK), Aarhus University (Denmark), and the European Film Academy.
